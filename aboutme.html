About me

Summary Statement

I am 25 years old and am currently interested in pursuing a career in computer programming. I have a degree in physics and math and enjoy solving problems that involve a mix of creativity and quantitative skills. I work well in a team and value being in an environment that emphasizes communication and outside the box thinking.

Work Experience

  1. Physics/Math Tutor, Self-Employed (01/16-05/16).

    I tutored a mix of Portland State University and high school students in physics and math. My greatest strength was making complex topics accessible to lay people who did not have a strong background in the natural sciences.

  2. Insurance Salesman, Wes Prater Farmers Agency (08/15-12/15)

    I sold property and casualty insurance over the phone and in person for a small Farmers agency. I also helped servive existing customers with changing coverages, filing claims and paying bills.

  3. Physics/Math Tutor, Self-Employed (01/2015-06/2015)

    I tutored undergraduate physics students at the University of Oklahoma. I emphasized clear communication and being thoroughly knowledgable about the given topic.

  4. Graduate Research Assistant, University of Oklahoma Physics Dept. (06/2012-01/2015)

    I worked in the lab of Dr. Jim Shaffer assisting on experiments in the realm of cold atom physics and spectroscopy. I was assigned specifically to the Rydberg Atom Cavity QED experiment, where I tuned lasers, aligned optics, and built and calibrated equipment. I was exposed to programming here, primarily in C++, which we used to automate aspects of the data collection.

Why Programming?

What I enjoy most about programming is that I get to build something, but I am not constrained by many of the physical limitations that one encounters when trying to create an object in 'real' space. I also enjoy the mix of quantitative skills and creativity required to be a good programmer. Finally, I see it as a rapidly growing and in-demand profession, which makes it appealing as well.
